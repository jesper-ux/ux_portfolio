Goal & Purpose

Improve the overall user experience of finding, selecting, customizing and purchasing Desenio’s curated gallery walls.

Emphasize

I started with creating a flow chart over the Gallery Wall Inspiration rooms in order to get an idea of how the current flow and tasks are set up on the website in order for a user to fulfill a complete purchase. In the flow chart, I defined that the Gallery Wall Inspiration Rooms can be divided into 5 different tasks:

  1. Finding the Gallery Wall rooms
  2. Selecting a Gallery Wall
  3. Customizing the Gallery Wall
  4. Add it to the shopping bag
  5. Completing the purchase in checkout.
I then interviewed various stakeholders and a representative from their Customer Support team to gather important insights. In order to better understand their users problems, needs and wishes I conducted one user interview and a user test. I also got to take part of 5 pre-recorded user tests that had been conducted before I started my internship which were analyzed together with my own user tests and interviews.

Define

I noted down quotes and grouped them thematically in order to pin point important insights from the interviews and user tests which was put together as an Empathy Map Canvas. When I was more aware of the problems, needs and wishes from their users, I also set up a cognitive walkthrough where I segmented all the gain and the pain points that I personally could identify for each task, with the users needs in mind.

I then created a User Story Map where I segmented all the pain points and gain points I had identified from my research into the different tasks. As I was going to present my research for various stakeholders at the company, I also created a User Story Journey in order to better communicate the gain and the pain points that were identified from my research.

User Journeys

When the User Journey had been completed, I invited all the stakeholders to a meeting where I presented the findings I had gathered in my research, and finally a summary of the major pain points which should be the focus of a new design iteration of the Gallery Wall Inspiration Rooms.

Since their system currently doesn’t bundle items within a gallery wall in the checkout, it is not possible to track them or benchmark the conversion rates for them, so I determined that the bundle feature for sure was the most important part of the MVP so they could start benchmarking the sales numbers. Currently there is no way of tracking the sales of the gallery walls.

Slide from research presentation showing the major pain points identified
Ideate

After the meeting with the stakeholders, I started benchmarking other sites and services in order to find inspiration for designs that could solve the major pain points. I collected all the benchmarks I could find in a Miro board, and did a Crazy 8 in order to ideate various design suggestions that could be integrated together with their current design.

Benchmarking best practises in Miro First iteration of idea from Crazy 8

I had a meeting with one of the stakeholders in order to determine a MVP for the first re-design of the gallery wall. We decided that the following features would be included in a first re-design:

Finding the gallery walls
  • Include gallery walls in the search functionality to make it easier for users to find the page.
Selecting a gallery wall
  • Include an "Add gallery wall to wishlist"-functionality, to help users save walls they like.
  • Enable same filters as single posters have (color, themes, art style, size, format), to make it easier for users to find what they want.
  • Enable same sorting options as single posters have (popularity, lowest price, highest price, new releases
  • Display name and price on overview page.
  • Include a "Shop instagram"-functionality (curated by users) as their sister company Poster Store has in order to enhance user engagement to the site.
Customizing a gallery wall
  • Lock the possibility of adding rooms that are not fully stocked to the shopping bag, to make sure users do not order an incomplete gallery wall.
  • Include a better item overview to make it easier for users to see the full content of a gallery wall
  • Design an easier overview of how a size change would affect style of gallery wall.
  • Make it easier to see which items are out of stock.
  • Include a clearer Call To Action on items that are out of stock.
  • Fix major flaws in customization process (e.g. block combinations that do not match
  • Refreshing the site should save previous customization and not reset it
Shopping bag and checkout
  • Bundle items within a gallery wall in wishlist, shopping bag, checkout and order confirmation so that customers can see which gallery wall they've bought. Currently all products are added as individual items, which means it is not possible to track sales or inform clients on which gallery wall they have bought. This will open up for tracking sales numbers of Gallery Walls, for future benchmarking.

We decided to exclude an idea of a "hanging guide", that would help the users on how to mount the gallery wall at home. This will most probably be a part of a later iteration.

Prototype & Test

From the first hand drawn sprites I created out of ideating through Crazy 8, I decided to build an interactive lo-fi prototype through Balsamiq. I then let my superior and her colleague test the prototype and give me feedback on it.
